Cane Corso Standard

This standard reflects a practical, functional interpretation of the Cane Corso, based on real-life work, temperament, and structural balance.

General Appearance

Medium to large-sized molossoid dog. Strong, muscular, well-proportioned, without exaggeration. The body must be powerful but agile.

Head

Large and imposing, with a wide skull and well-defined stop. The muzzle is strong and deep, not narrow or pointed.

Temperament

Stable, confident, loyal. Naturally protective without unnecessary aggression. A Cane Corso must be reliable in family and working environments.

Movement

Free, powerful, and coordinated. Movement must reflect endurance and balance, not heaviness.

Disqualifying Faults

  • Unstable or aggressive temperament
  • Extreme structural exaggerations
  • Lack of breed type

This standard is used as a reference point and assisted by AI tools for evaluation, but final decisions are made by experienced human review.
